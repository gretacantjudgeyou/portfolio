Overview

In collaboration with Instituto Marangoni and Temera, we crafted an immersive three-day event in Paris that bridged the gap between fashion education and technological innovation. The event brought together industry leaders, students, and professionals to explore the future of fashion through the lens of digital transformation.

Our team was responsible for the end-to-end production, from conceptualization to execution, ensuring a seamless experience that exceeded expectations and delivered measurable impact for all stakeholders.

Challenge

Creating a cohesive experience that would resonate with both fashion students and tech professionals required a delicate balance. We needed to design an event that was both educational and inspirational, with content that was accessible to beginners yet valuable for industry veterans.

Additional challenges included coordinating international speakers, managing hybrid elements for remote participants, and creating networking opportunities that would foster meaningful connections.

Solution

We developed a multi-track program that included keynote presentations, interactive workshops, and networking sessions. The event was structured to allow participants to choose their own journey based on their interests and experience level.

Key elements included hands-on tech demonstrations, case study presentations from leading fashion brands, and mentorship sessions with industry experts. We also implemented a dedicated event app to facilitate networking and provide real-time updates to attendees.

Results

The event was a resounding success, with 98% of attendees rating it as "excellent" or "very good" in post-event surveys. The partnership between Instituto Marangoni and Temera was strengthened, leading to ongoing collaborations and the establishment of a new scholarship program for students interested in fashion technology.

Follow-up surveys conducted three months after the event showed that 85% of participants had implemented learnings from the event in their work or studies, and 72% reported forming valuable professional connections that they maintained after the event.
